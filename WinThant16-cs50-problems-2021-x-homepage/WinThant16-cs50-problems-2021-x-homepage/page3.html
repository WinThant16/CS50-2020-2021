Here, we will explore Turtwig and its evolution.

Turtwig is a small, light green Pokémon resembling a tortoise. It has a small brown shell on its body that is made up of Earth. Very healthy Turtwigs have moist shells. Turtwigs usually nourish themselves through photosynthesis. Drinking water will also help harden its soil-based shell.

Grotle has a large shell covering its back. It extends from its forehead to the tip of its short tail. There are also bush-like trees growing on its shell. Grotles have the ability to see where pure water wells up, and will help carry Pokémon on its back towards them. Grotles live in forests but leave during the day to allow more sunlight on their trees.

Torterra is a tall bulky Pokémon resembling a tortoise. Its large and clean shells support the lives of many small Pokémon, which occasionally gather and build their nests on its shell. Torterras migrate in search of water, creating the illusion of "moving forests".

They are all strong against Water, Rock and Ground Pokémons.

Turtwigs and Grotles are Grass-type Pokémons and weak against Pokémons with Fire, Ice, Poison, Flying and Bug moves.

Torterras are dual-type Grass/Ground Pokémons, and weak additionally weak against Flying and Bug Pokémons.
